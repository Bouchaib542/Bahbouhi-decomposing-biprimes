Validation of Goldbach's Conjecture Using the Hybrid GPS Method

Introduction

The Goldbach Conjecture asserts that every even number greater than 2 can be written as the sum of two prime numbers: E = p + q. This conjecture, proposed in 1742, remains unproven despite centuries of computational verification and theoretical interest.

The GPS Hybrid Method

The GPS (Guided Prime Search) method is an algorithmic strategy that predicts pairs (p, q) such that p + q = E. The hybrid version combines modular arithmetic insights, exponential navigation, and structural properties of primes to avoid brute force.

Given a large even number E, the method explores values of p = E/2 - t and q = E/2 + t such that both p and q are prime. The search is guided by theoretical filters and optimized steps based on prior tests and known patterns.

Conclusion

The hybrid GPS method provides a new path to understanding and exploring the Goldbach Conjecture. By combining number theory, computation, and predictive strategies, we demonstrate a scalable way to navigate the conjecture beyond previously reachable limits.
